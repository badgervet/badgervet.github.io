Bash Script Crazy at USITCC Nationals

Springfield, Missouri – The 2025 United States Information Technology Collegiate Conference (USITCC), a prestigious national event, brought together 170 participants, including students and sponsors, for a showcase of technical talent and innovation. Among the standout performers were five students from Amarillo College, representing the organization Bash Script Crazy, under the leadership of John Emery.

The team, consisting of Jackson George, Xander Galusha, Ezri King, Efren King, and Kyle Cooper, demonstrated exceptional skill across multiple categories, earning a total of four honorable mentions and additional strong finishes in the top 25.

In the PC Troubleshooting category, Kyle Cooper and Xander Galusha both secured Honorable Mention awards, placing them among the top 10 competitors nationwide. Their ability to diagnose and resolve complex technical issues under pressure highlighted their expertise and teamwork.

The Security category saw even more success for Bash Script Crazy, with Kyle Cooper and Jackson George each earning Honorable Mention awards placing them in the Top 10 as well. Additionally, Efren King and Xander Galusha finished in the top 25, further showcasing the group’s depth of talent in safeguarding systems against cyber threats.

John Emery, the team’s mentor, expressed pride in their accomplishments. “These students worked tirelessly to prepare, and their results reflect not only their technical abilities but also their dedication and passion for IT,” Emery said. “Competing at a national level against such a large and talented field is no small feat.”

The 2025 USITCC Student Conference provided a platform for students to test their skills, network with peers, and gain recognition in the IT community. For the Bash Script Crazy team from Amarillo College, the event marked a triumphant showing, with multiple awards and a promising outlook for their future in the field.
